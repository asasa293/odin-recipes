Spaghetti All'Assassina

Don't worry about the burnt parts. This is intentional and a characteristic part of this recipe.

Ingredients

  • Spaguetti
  • Canned tomatoes
  • Tomato paste
  • Peperoncino
  • Olive oil
  • Sliced garlic

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Cook the spaghetti according to the package instructions until al dente. Reserve a cup of pasta cooking water, then drain the spaghetti.
  2. While the spaghetti is cooking, heat a couple of tablespoons of olive oil in a large skillet over medium heat. Add the diced pancetta and cook until it starts to render its fat and become crispy.
  3. In a separate large skillet, heat a couple of tablespoons of olive oil over medium-high heat.
  4. Once the oil is hot, add the cooked spaghetti to the skillet in a single layer. Allow it to cook without stirring for a few minutes until it starts to develop a golden-brown crust on the bottom.
  5. Using tongs or a spatula, carefully flip sections of the spaghetti to evenly toast the other side. Be cautious as the spaghetti may splatter.
  6. Continue cooking the spaghetti, flipping occasionally, until it is evenly golden and crispy on the outside while still tender on the inside. This step adds a unique texture and flavor to the dish.
  7. Once the spaghetti is toasted to your liking, proceed with the original recipe by adding it to the skillet with the sauce and tossing to combine.
  8. Serve the spaghetti all'assassina hot, garnished with grated pecorino romano cheese and chopped fresh parsley.
